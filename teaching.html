My Core Beliefs

For me, teaching is more than just sharing knowledge. It is about helping students become confident and curious thinkers. I believe every student should feel heard and motivated to take charge of their own learning. I work to build real relationships so students feel safe to take risks and make mistakes, since that is when real growth happens.

I think effective teaching starts with understanding each student’s background, interests, and goals. I try to adjust my lessons so every student, no matter their ability, can connect with mathematical ideas. Collaboration, reflection, and helpful feedback are key parts of my teaching. I use them to help students turn content into real understanding.

As a teacher, I see myself as a lifelong learner. I stay open to feedback, use new technology, and keep improving my teaching methods so I can stay current and meet students’ needs. My teaching is based on empathy, consistency, and clarity. These are the same values I want my students to develop.

  • Adjust teaching methods to support different learning needs.
  • Encourage a growth mindset and resilience by giving helpful feedback.
  • Design lessons that connect math to real-life situations.
  • Build a classroom where respect, teamwork, and curiosity are encouraged.
